# Hypothesis And Evidence

Analytical research should separate facts from explanations.

## Evidence labels

Use these labels explicitly:

- `observed`
- `inferred`
- `unverified`

## Hypothesis quality

A useful hypothesis:
- explains the observed movement
- can be checked with a small number of cuts
- has a plausible business mechanism
- can in principle be falsified

A weak hypothesis:
- is vague
- cannot be tested cleanly
- just restates the metric movement in different words

## Common explanation families

- demand composition changed
- supply availability changed
- market mix changed
- product behavior changed
- price or incentive changes affected behavior
- experiment or rollout affected a subset
- pipeline or measurement changed
- seasonality or calendar effects distorted the baseline

## Interpretation rules

- do not rank hypotheses confidently without evidence
- prefer "most consistent with current evidence" over "caused by"
- if multiple explanations remain plausible, say that
- if a data-quality issue can explain the movement, keep it near the top until ruled out
