# Statistical Interpretation

Statistical testing should support a decision, not just produce a p-value.

## Always report

- direction of effect
- approximate effect size
- uncertainty
- practical relevance
- key assumptions or caveats

## Interpretation rules

- statistical significance is not practical significance
- non-significance is not proof of no effect
- a narrow but tiny effect may not matter operationally
- a large but noisy effect may still justify follow-up

## Preferred language

- "consistent with"
- "suggests"
- "inconclusive"
- "not precise enough yet"
- "directionally positive/negative, but uncertain"

Avoid:

- "proved"
- "definitely caused"
- "no effect" when evidence is merely weak

## Escalation rule

If the result is decision-critical and uncertainty remains high:
- recommend more data
- recommend a cleaner design
- or recommend a narrower decision claim
