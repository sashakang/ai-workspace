# Research Framing

Use analytical research when the user is asking "what is going on?" rather than "pull this exact dataset."

## Good research prompts

- why did conversion drop last week
- what likely drove revenue growth in one region but not another
- what changed after a pricing or product launch
- where should we look first for a funnel regression

## Framing rules

- reduce the question to one focal metric or behavior first
- define the comparison window explicitly
- define the comparison group explicitly
- name the business decision the research should support

## Preferred structure

State these before exploring explanations:
- focal metric
- comparison baseline
- affected segment
- suspected incident or change window
- confidence level in the observed movement itself

## Escalation rule

If the question is too broad to answer responsibly in one pass:
- narrow the scope
- split the work into phases
- or propose the first discriminating cuts instead of pretending to have the answer
